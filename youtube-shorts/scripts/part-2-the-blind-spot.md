# Part 2 — The Blind Spot
**Tone:** Opportunity / Revelation
**Hook:** How did this happen — and what does fixing it look like?

---

## HeyGen Script

**[Conversational. Like a colleague sharing intel.]**

"So how does a $76 trillion market get overlooked?"

**[Pause.]**

"It's not malicious. It's momentum."

"Marketing systems got built around younger demographics."

"The tools, the metrics, the assumptions —"

"all optimized for a customer who wasn't 50."

**[Beat.]**

"And nobody stopped to ask — what are we missing?"

**[Shift — now forward-looking.]**

"Here's what the data actually shows."

"Adults 50 and older buy more cars per capita than 18 to 34 year olds."

"They spend more on travel. More on home improvement."

"They are the **largest** purchasers of technology in the US."

**[Pause. Let that land.]**

"The largest purchasers of technology."

**[Direct. Warm.]**

"The organizations that figure this out first —"

"that build real relationships with this community —"

"own this market for the next 20 years."

**[Final line.]**

"50+TechBridge is already building those relationships."

"The question is — are you?"

**[Hold.]**

---

## Delivery Notes

| Moment | Direction |
|---|---|
| "It's not malicious. It's momentum." | Generous — you're giving them an out |
| "Nobody stopped to ask" | Curious, not accusatory |
| The spending stats | Confidence — these are facts, not arguments |
| "Largest purchasers of technology" | Repeat it. Let it echo. |
| "Own this market for 20 years" | This is the hook for every business brain in the room |
| "Are you?" | Quiet. Direct. Leave it hanging. |
