## The Honest Assessment

**Book A** gets you on the TED stage.
**Book B** builds the business.

They are not competitors. They are **sequence.**

---

## Recommended Path

### Write Book B first.
- Faster to write (practical, not memoir)
- Generates revenue while you write Book A
- Builds the community that becomes the audience for Book A
- The stories in Book B (Sandra, Patricia, Dorothy, Frank) become
  the "people" chapters in Book A
- Proves the model — so Book A has receipts

### Then write Book A.
- Brian's story is now backed by a published book, a community,
  and a full business
- The TED application has a credential stack
- Traditional publishers have sales data to look at

**Book B = the engine.**
**Book A = the flag.**

---

## OR — The Hybrid Option

**Combine them into one book.**

Structure:
- Open with Brian's story (Book A intro + memoir chapters)
- Each chapter pairs a personal/advocacy moment with a practical skill
- Example: Chapter 1 opens with the AARP disillusionment moment →
  then teaches AI as the solution the old system refused to use

This is harder to write but is the most powerful single book.
It's what the master outline was gesturing toward.
