If you run a workforce development program, you already know the pressure.

Serve more people. Show better outcomes. Do it with the same budget. Report quarterly.

Here's what most programs are doing: nothing specific for adults 50+.

### WIOA Was Built for This Moment

The Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act specifically prioritizes:

- **Adults facing barriers to employment** — age discrimination qualifies
- **Individuals with skills gaps** — digital and AI literacy qualifies
- **Training providers on the Eligible Training Provider List** — we're working toward ETPL listing
- **Programs that demonstrate measurable outcomes** — 3X completion rate, 74% confidence increase

WIOA Individual Training Accounts (ITAs) can fund AI and digital skills training for adults 50+ in your service area. The mechanism already exists. The funding already exists.

What hasn't existed — until now — is a training program designed specifically for this population that actually produces results.

### Why Generic Training Fails Adults 50+

The average digital skills program was designed for a 28-year-old who grew up with smartphones.

The pace is wrong. The language is wrong. The assumptions about prior knowledge are wrong.

When a 60-year-old sits in a workshop titled "Introduction to AI" alongside 25-year-olds who've been using ChatGPT for two years, the outcome is predictable.

They feel behind before the instructor opens their mouth.

They sit quietly. They don't ask questions. They leave early. They don't come back.

And your program reports another non-completion.

This is not because adults 50+ can't learn AI.

It's because the training wasn't built for them.
